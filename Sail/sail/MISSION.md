# COMMUNICATION STANDARD

## The Sail Voice
Everything we send — emails, pitches, WhatsApp messages, social posts — follows ONE consistent voice.

### References (M has established these):
1. **Pitch deck style** — Arabic, 8 slides, Gamma format. Direct, visual, emotional.
2. **Y Combinator approach** — lead with the problem, show the solution working, make the ask clear
3. **High-value email template** — strong subject line, immediate value in first line, clear CTA, makes them feel stupid NOT to respond (reference: email screenshot M shared)
4. **Profile/brand presence** — professional but not corporate. Saudi but not traditional. Tech but not intimidating.

### Communication Rules
1. **Open with pain, not features.** "You're losing X hours/riyals" beats "We offer AI agents"
2. **CTA is mandatory.** Every message ends with a clear next step
3. **Personalized > templated.** Use their business name, their specific problem
4. **Short > long.** Respect their time. If it takes 30 seconds to read, it's too long.
5. **Arabic colloquial for Saudi market.** English only for international/enterprise
6. **Social proof early.** "We did X for Y" — even if Y is your own business
7. **Y Combinator energy** — be the startup that's clearly going somewhere, not the vendor begging for a meeting

### Pitch Structure (for any medium)
1. **Hook** — their pain point, stated better than they could
2. **Agitate** — what it costs them (time, money, sleep, growth)
3. **Solution** — what Sail does, in one sentence
4. **Proof** — demo, case study, or "here's it working right now"
5. **CTA** — one clear action (book demo, reply, call)
